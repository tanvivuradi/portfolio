Designing MACS Courses for BC Pharmacists

This project involved the design and development of online courses for pharmacists in British Columbia to expand their scope of practice in treating minor ailments and prescribing contraceptives.

Project Overview

Objective: Enable pharmacists to deliver enhanced healthcare services by providing accessible, high-quality online courses.

Collaborators: Subject matter experts from the UBC Faculty of Pharmaceutical Sciences and the Office of Education Technology and Learning Design (OETLD).

Tools Used:

  • Learning Management System (LMS): Canvas
  • Collaboration Tools: SharePoint, Microsoft Teams, Outlook, Zoom
  • Content Creation Software: Articulate, Camtasia, Vyond, Adobe Creative Suite, H5P
  • Survey Tools: Qualtrics for learner feedback collection, Excel

    • Learning Design Proposal: Structuring the Course for Success

    The Learning Design Proposal served as a blueprint for course development, outlining content structure, multimedia integration, and instructional strategies. This document ensured alignment with learning objectives, SME expertise, and best practices in online education.

    Needs Analysis & SME Collaboration

    Before developing the course, I conducted a needs analysis to identify key pharmacist training gaps and ensure alignment with healthcare regulations. This involved reviewing existing resources, analyzing learning requirements, and gathering insights from stakeholders. Once the objectives were defined, I coordinated with SMEs, instructional designers, and project leads to refine the content and instructional approach. To maintain consistency and engagement, I facilitated a kick-off meeting with SMEs, outlining: Project goals and learning objectives Course structure and multimedia integration plan Development timeline and key milestones

    Action Mapping: Defining Key Learning Actions

    To ensure the course directly supported pharmacists' real-world responsibilities, I used Action Mapping to align content with practical application. The key focus areas included:
  • Collect – Gathering patient information and assessing symptoms
  • Assess – Evaluating eligibility for minor ailment treatment
  • Plan – Determining the best course of action for prescribing medication
  • Implement – Applying knowledge to real-world scenarios through interactive cases
  • Monitor – Following up with patients and ensuring treatment effectiveness

    • Course Blueprint & Development

    With the structure defined, I developed the Learning Design Proposal, which included:
  • Course Structure: Organizing content into digestible, well-paced modules
  • Storyboarding: Outlining text-based and visual elements to ensure engagement
  • Multimedia Planning: Integrating videos, animations, and interactive case studies to enhance retention
  • Once the proposal was finalized, I began the LMS development phase, building out modules in Canvas LMS and implementing interactive elements like:
    • Scenario-based decision-making activities
    • Drag-and-drop learning exercises
    • Multimedia content for diverse learning preferences

    Prototype & Testing

    To ensure usability and effectiveness, I developed a prototype module and gathered SME and stakeholder feedback. This iterative process allowed me to refine content structure, adjust interactive elements, and improve accessibility.

    Review & Quality Assurance

    Before the final launch, the course underwent expert reviews and usability testing. SMEs validated content accuracy, while instructional designers ensured that learning objectives were met. After multiple revisions, the course was finalized and prepared for deployment.

    Launch, Implementation & Evaluation

    The course was successfully launched, providing over 2,500 pharmacists across BC with essential training. I implemented learner feedback mechanisms using Qualtrics surveys and performance data analysis to continuously improve the course. Based on feedback, I refined content, adjusted interactivity, and enhanced accessibility to ensure an optimal learning experience.

    Impact & Results

    This project significantly improved pharmacist training by providing a structured, interactive, and practical learning experience. The course not only expanded pharmacists’ ability to prescribe minor ailment treatments and contraceptives but also contributed to improved patient care accessibility across BC.
  • Improved learner engagement through interactive content and multimedia
  • Strengthened 2,500+ pharmacist readiness for real-world application
