eLearning Design Project: Expanding Pharmacist Services in B.C.

This project involved the development of eLearning courses for pharmacists in British Columbia to expand their scope of practice for 21 minor ailments and contraception. As part of this government-funded initiative, I contributed to the instructional design of 8 out of 21 courses, providing an engaging and learner-centered experience.

Project Overview

Objective: Enable pharmacists to deliver enhanced healthcare services by providing accessible, high-quality online courses that train them in treating minor ailments and prescribing contraceptives.

Collaborators: Subject matter experts from the UBC Faculty of Pharmaceutical Sciences and the Office of Education Technology and Learning Design (OETLD).

Tools Used: SharePoint for collaboration and Canvas for course implementation.

Instructional Design Approach

  • Incorporated expert feedback through iterative design reviews and collaborative check-ins.
  • Applied learning design principles to structure course content, quizzes, and case studies.
  • Created interactive elements to simulate real-world decision-making for pharmacists.

User-Centered Enhancements

  • Analyzed post-course surveys to refine content and improve user experience.
  • Enhanced content accessibility and engagement by aligning with best practices in eLearning design.

Outcomes and Impact

  • Expanded learning resources for over 2,500 pharmacists across British Columbia.
  • Increased pharmacist readiness for prescribing minor ailment treatments and contraceptives, enhancing patient care accessibility.
