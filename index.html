Research Highlights

I co-authored a research paper titled "How Should a Social Robot Deliver Negative Feedback Without Creating Distance Between the Robot and Child Users?" , published in the proceedings of HAI 2023.

Summary

  • Objective: Investigated children’s preferences for social robot facial expressions to deliver feedback effectively.
  • Key Findings:
    • Children preferred friendly and non-punitive facial expressions over angry or fear-inducing ones.
    • Positive expressions encouraged better engagement and acceptance as a study companion.
    • 60% of children expressed interest in using the robot for learning tasks.
  • Impact: These findings contribute to the design of child-friendly robots that support motivation and focus in learning environments.
